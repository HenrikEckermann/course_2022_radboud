Multi-omic data science with R/Bioconductor

Chapter 3 Tentative Program

This course is based on data science with R, a popular open source environment for scientific data analysis. It provides general capabilities for the analysis, integration, and visualization of multi-omic data from biomedical studies.

Course objectives The course aims to provide the basic understanding and skills for biomedical data analysis with R. The course provides an overview of reproducible data analysis and reporting in multi-omics, with recent example data sets published microbiome studies and other biomedical data, considering the identified needs of the registered participants. The students will become familiar with standard concepts and methods in biomedical data analysis. After the course the students will know how to approach new data analytical tasks with R.

The course takes place over one work week, Monday to Friday, from 9am – 5pm. Coffee and lunch breaks will be scheduled between sessions. We will consider the possibility for additional evening program. The program is tentative, and will be designed in more detail after the proposal acceptance. We will pay particular attention to the needs of the participants from the local research community in Oulu.

The course will be organized in a hybrid format, and all students will have a possibility for contact teaching. A priority will given for local students from Oulu. In addition, a competitive international call for participation will be organized for PhD students, with an option for distant attendance. The hybrid format will also allow us to update the overall plan in a flexible way depending on the pandemic situation, if necessary.

Following the structure that we have used in other similar courses, the mornings will be dedicated to lectures, and afternoons for the hands-on sessions that consist of practical tasks and example data from recent research literature. Students will solve the exercises by taking advantage of the online examples and resources that are pointed out in the study material. There is often more than one way to solve a given task, and the teachers will be available for assistance.

Monday:

  • Orientation
  • Best practices in reproducible reporting and open science
  • Hands-on: Introduction to R

Tuesday:

  • Key concepts and challenges in biomedical data analysis
  • Hands-on: Biomedical data exploration

Wednesday:

  • Hands-on: Biomedical data visualization

Thursday:

  • Advanced topics: common machine learning techniques
  • Hands-on: multi-omic data integration and reproducible workflows

Friday:

  • Student presentations
  • Summary & Conclusions

3.1 Material

The teaching material follows open online documentation created by the course teachers, extending the online book Orchestrating Microbiome Analysis (https://microbiome.github.io/OMA). We will teach generic data analytical skills that are applicable to common data analysis tasks encountered in modern omics research.

The training material walks through example workflows that go through standard steps of biomedical data analysis covering data access, exploration, analysis, visualization, reproducible reporting, and best practices in open science. The teaching format allows adaptations according to the student’s learning speed.

You can run the workflow by simply copy-pasting the examples. For further, advanced material, you can test and modify further examples from the OMA book, or try to apply the techniques to your own data.

We expect that the students will install the necessary software in advance. Online support will be available.
